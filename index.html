Educational Toolbox

\

Understanding these biological concepts helps you interrupt maladaptive cycles [1]:

\
    \
  • The HPA Axis: Stress activates the Hypothalamus to release CRH, leading to Cortisol (CORT) from the adrenal cortex. CORT mobilizes energy but increases cravings for high-energy "comfort" foods [2, 3].
    • \
  • Insulin & Storage: Insulin is the sole hormone for anabolic energy storage. Chronic stress can cause insulin insensitivity, leading to "Type 2 Diabetes" where glucose stays in the blood and becomes toxic [4-6].
    • \
  • Leptin vs. Ghrelin: Ghrelin peaks at meal times to stimulate hunger, while Leptin (produced by fat cells) inhibits hunger. Stress can oppose leptin signaling, making you feel hungry even when energy stores are full [7-9].
    • \
  • SIRT1 & Resilience: Caloric restriction activates SIRT1, which improves insulin sensitivity and may extend lifespan [10, 11].
    • \
