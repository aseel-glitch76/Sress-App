HPA Axis & Cortisol

\

Stress triggers the Hypothalamic-Pituitary-Adrenal (HPA) axis, releasing Cortisol (CORT). CORT mobilizes glucose for "fight or flight" but also increases cravings for high-energy foods [1, 2].

\
\
\

Leptin vs. Ghrelin

\

Ghrelin is an orexigenic peptide released by the stomach to signal energy needs. Leptin, produced by fat cells, is an anorexigenic signal that promotes satiety [3, 4].

\
\
\

Insulin Resistance

\

Insulin is the sole hormone for energy storage. Chronic stress or high-sugar intake can lead to insulin insensitivity, contributing to metabolic disorders and hyperphagia [4, 5].

\
\
\

The Stress-Eating Link

\

CORT inhibits the CRH feedback loop, leading to cravings. Eating acts as a negative feedback mechanism on the HPA axis to cope with stress, but often leads to abdominal fat deposition [6, 7].
